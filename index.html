Benjamin Graham Rules (Intelligent Investor):
PE < 15 = undervalued | PB < 1.5 = below book | Graham Number = sqrt(22.5 x EPS x BV) | Buy below Graham Number | Margin of Safety = never pay full price

Warren Buffett Rules (Letters to Shareholders):
ROE > 15% consistently = quality business | Debt/Equity < 0.5 = strong balance sheet | Buy great business at fair price, not fair business at great price | Economic moat = pricing power

Peter Lynch Rules (One Up On Wall Street):
PEG < 1 = growth at reasonable price | PEG = PE / annual growth rate | PEG < 0.5 = excellent value | Know what you own and why

John Templeton Rules:
Buy at maximum pessimism | Stock 40%+ below 52W high = Templeton zone | Bull markets born in pessimism, grow in skepticism

Joel Greenblatt Magic Formula:
High ROE + Low PE = best combination | Rank stocks by ROE and PE separately, combine ranks

Philip Fisher Rules (Common Stocks):
Revenue growth > 15% = superior business | Management quality proxy = sustained high ROE | Scuttlebutt = industry checks
